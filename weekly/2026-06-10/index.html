Decision · LaunchDarkly

We're in shape to start moving traffic to Flow B.

34 days post-100%, 4,819 new devices. Flow B is at worst neutral and clearly better on friction + duplicate-prevention. No regressions on anything material.

  • Proposal: ramp B to 75% for a week, watch the scope-leak + completion, then go 100%.
  • The win is less friction + fewer duplicate accounts, not raw completion — that converged to a tie.
Why now

The metrics have been flat for 5 straight refreshes. More waiting won't change the call — it just delays the friction win.

Clarified · accidental indie signups

Not ours to move — they come through a different door.

Last week, reading the CS excel, accidental signups looked flat at ~3-5/week. Looking closer this week: they don't go through our login experiment at all.

  • 0 indie accounts were created carrying an experiment variant — indies sign up via a separate onboarding surface, not Flow A/B.
  • By signup date they're steady ~3-5/week (CS). The May 18 "spike" was the inc-264 fraud accounts caught by our internal proxy — not real signups, and resolved.
  • What is ours: Flow B preventing duplicate shop-barber accounts (the 5.6% vs 19.4% above). Don't conflate the two.
